About Leon

HCI | AR/VR | ML | Haptics | CV

Leon is a senior undergraduate at Florida National University, majoring in Information Technology with a concentration in Software Engineering. His projects and research interests span Haptic Technologies, Ubiquitous Computing, Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), and Accessibility Technologies, particularly within the XR ecosystem (Virtual, Augmented, and Mixed Reality).


Currently, Leon is a research intern at the MIT Media Lab under Dr. Danielle Wood, where he explores Human-Robot Interaction in the International Space Station with a focus on Computer Vision. He is also collaborating with the University of Central Florida's ISUE Lab under Dr. Joseph LaViola, leading a team in examining situational awareness with multi-angle sensor integration for head-mounted VR displays.


Leon has professional experience through software engineering internships at What If Media Group, Sustainible, and Instrument, where he contributed to the development of the Google Design and Microsoft Unlocked websites.


He is passionate about fostering community and has actively participated in local, national, and international hackathons. Leon serves as the president of the FNU Tech Club and is recognized as a GitHub Campus Expert. Beyond academics and technology, Leon is also a committed college student-athlete.
